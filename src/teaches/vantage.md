# Vantage

### Overview
- [ ] Theme & vibe: sci-fi open-world exploration
- [ ] Objective: complete a **mission** (mission victory), a **destiny** (destiny victory), or both (epic victory)
- [ ] Game end: mission completed, destiny completed, or any player's time/morale/health hits 0

### Location Cards
- [ ] Each player has their own location card in a card holder — **no one else may look at it**
- [ ] You can describe the art and read the text/actions aloud; you just can't show it
- [ ] Your location = your "vantage"; other players are elsewhere on the planet
- [ ] Location number (top left) is used to look up storybook entries
- [ ] Compass (top right) shows 4 directions you can depart
  - [ ] Listed number = adjacent location, 1-cost move with no storybook
  - [ ] Star (*) = must look up cost in Depart storybook before committing
  - [ ] No number or * = blocked direction
- [ ] Right sidebar = your action options (usually 6)
- [ ] Triangle icon = mandatory effect on arrival (typically suffer 1 heat/cold/hypoxia damage)
- [ ] Traveler icon + quotes = an NPC is talking to you

### Skills
- [ ] 6 types matching the 6 action categories: move, look, engage, help, take, overpower
- [ ] Use to reduce action cost (any player can help); also gained/spent as action results

### Taking a Turn (8 Steps)
- [ ] **1. Choose an action** (location, card, or depart) — you commit before knowing the cost
  - [ ] Can't change your mind after proceeding to step 2
  - [ ] **Location action**: pick one from the sidebar; reference matching storybook category + location number
    - [ ] Only 1 location action per location per game (unless "always available" is noted)
    - [ ] Don't do something thematically contradicted by another player's prior visit
  - [ ] **Card action**: use an action on a card in your grid, supply, or the center; reference that card's number in the matching storybook
  - [ ] **Depart**: move in one of 4 directions (move action)
- [ ] **2. Read cost and action** (not the result) — another player reads from the storybook
- [ ] **3. Reduce cost** by 1 per matching skill token, paid by any player
  - [ ] Any player can contribute skill tokens to help anyone at any time
  - [ ] Discard paid tokens to general supply
- [ ] **4. Roll challenge dice** from the pool equal to remaining cost
  - [ ] If not enough dice in pool, first refresh ALL dice (from grids + penalty section) back to pool
- [ ] **5. Place dice** on open slots in your grid (or impact slots on any player's grid)
  - [ ] Placing a die avoids its penalty
  - [ ] Each slot holds 1 die; slots have restrictions (skill type, ability, creature type, terrain, specific face)
  - [ ] Impact slots: any player may use these with your permission, even from afar
  - [ ] If another player is at your location, you may use their non-impact slots too (with permission)
  - [ ] Arrow pointing away from slot = bonus when you place a die there (usually boost)
  - [ ] Arrow pointing toward slot = you must pay boost to use that slot
- [ ] **6. Suffer penalties** from unplaced dice → lose time/morale/health shown; discard to penalty section
  - [ ] Setback result: return to pool instead of penalty section (delays refresh)
  - [ ] Blank result: no penalty; just discard to penalty section
  - [ ] Losses applied simultaneously
- [ ] **7. Read the action result** — another player reads the storybook result aloud
  - [ ] Multi-option results: make your choice *before* hearing the outcome
  - [ ] Heat/cold/hypoxia damage = lose 1 health OR both 1 time + 1 morale (your choice)
  - [ ] If a stat would exceed 6, gain 1 random skill instead
- [ ] **8. End your turn** → play passes clockwise
  - [ ] "Continue" in result = must perform another action (can choose a different location action)
  - [ ] "You may continue" = optional
  - [ ] Continue only applies to current turn

### Boost
- [ ] Boost (cubes) live on your cards; spending and gaining happens per-card
  - [ ] Exception: boost from location actions goes on/from your character card
- [ ] Use boost powers at *any time*, even mid-action or on another player's turn
- [ ] Each card has a boost capacity — can't exceed it
- [ ] Effects last until end of current turn

### Your Grid
- [ ] 3×3 tableau; your character always goes in the middle
- [ ] Gain vertical cards → place in any open space; can't reposition once placed
- [ ] Cards provide extra dice slots, boost powers, reserve capacity, and other abilities
- [ ] Grid benefits only apply to your own grid (except impact slots/powers)
- [ ] First player to fill all 9 slots → place destiny card 1705 in center
- [ ] Replacing/losing a card: refresh its dice, return its boost to supply, return card to box

### Supply & Reserve
- [ ] Supply: horizontal cards (items, flora, vehicles, missions, destinies); no limit; can't place challenge dice on them
- [ ] Reserve: vertical cards kept outside your grid; capacity = total ✦ icons on your grid/supply cards
- [ ] Reserved cards: can't use slots/abilities/powers, but retain boost; challenge dice on them refresh
- [ ] Each character has a boost power to pull a reserved card into your grid (no "when placed" benefit)

### Duplicate Cards
- [ ] Gain a card already in your grid → gain 1 boost on that card instead (if possible)
- [ ] Gain a card another player has → both players decide who keeps it; loser gets 1 boost on character

### End of Game
- [ ] Complete mission → choose to end or continue pursuing destiny
- [ ] Complete destiny → read its storybook entry to end game
- [ ] Any stat hits 0 → read take storybook (may be able to continue)
